## The dataset hook (`sensor_readings`)

- **Location (clean asset, not a submission):**
  `~/working/teaching/courses/linalg/assessments/project-1/datasets/sensor_readings.csv`.
  Read directly; **never read any per-student dir under `assessments/<NNN>/`** (PII).
- **What it actually is.** Generated by `generate_scale_varying_data` in
  `project-1/generate_datasets.py`: 1000 samples x 10 features of i.i.d. standard
  Gaussian noise, each column multiplied by `np.logspace(-2, 2, 10)`. Verified
  empirically: per-column std runs 0.01, 0.028, 0.076, 0.21, 0.61, 1.73, 4.78,
  13.3, 35.4, 94.5 (the logspace scale ladder). Columns are **independent** by
  construction (no cross-correlation).
- **What it was for.** In project-1 it is the *scale-varying* dataset, used to
  demonstrate normalization/standardization effects in SVD/PCA covariance
  estimation. project-1's "estimation" is **covariance-estimation-via-SVD**, not
  linear MMSE. So the raw asset carries **no X-from-Y signal-plus-noise structure**.
- **Implication.** The Ch 12 capstone must **impose** an estimation problem on this
  matrix rather than read one off it. Two clean options:
  1. Designate a subset of columns as observed measurements `Y` and a held-out
     column (or a constructed latent) as the target `X`, then estimate `X` from `Y`
     via the sample cross-covariance plug-in LMMSE. As shipped the columns are
     independent, so a defensible capstone re-generates or augments the data with a
     **known signal + measurement-noise model** (`Y = HX + noise`) so the LMMSE
     estimate is non-trivial and its error covariance is checkable against truth.
  2. Keep `sensor_readings` purely as the *named, recurring* dataset (continuity
     into Ch 13-14) and inject the signal/noise covariance the estimator needs.
  Either way the dataset is a **seed/continuity asset, not turnkey source**.
- **Why this dataset.** It is the single named asset for the entire Ch 12 -> Ch 13
  -> Ch 14 arc (per `coverage-by-chapter.md`): the static LMMSE estimator built
  here becomes the recursive estimator in Ch 13 and the full Kalman filter in
  Ch 14 ("the Estimator seed ... finale"). Continuity of dataset across the three
  chapters is the reason to anchor 12.6 on it even though it needs re-engineering.

## In-book seeds this chapter pays off (the real "source")

Because there is no external source, Ch 12's load-bearing inputs are earlier
chapters of this book. These must already be written/consistent for Ch 12 to land:

- **Ch 5.4 "The best predictor"** (`outline` line 91, tagged **MMSE seed**): the
  conditional mean `E[X|Y]` minimizes MSE. Ch 12 is the linear-restricted,
  covariance-only version of that result. 12.1 and 12.4 cash this in.
- **Ch 7.4 "The conditional Gaussian"** (`outline` line 110, tagged **LMMSE
  seed**): the conditional mean is *linear* in the conditioning variable, with
  conditional covariance independent of the value observed. This is exactly why
  **12.4** can prove LMMSE = MMSE = conditional mean in the Gaussian case.
- **Ch 6.4 Cross-correlation / cross-covariance**: supplies the
  `C_XY`, `C_YY` objects the 12.3 derivation is written in. Notation source, not
  theory source.
- **Ch 11.2 geometry of least squares (projection / orthogonal residual)**: the
  orthogonality principle (12.2) is the random-vector analogue. Ch 12 should
  reference, not re-teach, that geometry; Ch 11 ships first in Part III.

## Gaps and conflicts

- **Whole chapter is net-new.** No lesson (L20 is title-only), no case study, no
  repo chapter. All seven sections are write-from-blank; "scratch" is accurate and,
  unlike Ch 2's mislabeled "strong", there is genuinely nothing to adapt.
- **Dataset/estimator mismatch.** `sensor_readings` was built as a PCA scaling demo
  with *independent* columns. It does not contain a ready estimation problem; 12.6
  must construct one (signal + measurement-noise model), or the LMMSE estimate is
  degenerate (zero cross-covariance -> estimator collapses to the prior mean).
  Flag for the chapter author: decide the `Y = HX + v` setup once, here, and carry
  the same generative model into Ch 13/14 so the Kalman arc is continuous.
- **Dependency risk.** Ch 12 cannot be drafted convincingly before Ch 5.4 and Ch
  7.4 exist, since its two headline results are payoffs of those seeds. Both of
  those chapters are themselves **scratch** (5) / **partial** (7). Sequence Ch 12
  after the seeds are at least outlined.
- **No conflicts** to reconcile (nothing to conflict with). The only "conflict" is
  the dataset's intended use vs. the use Ch 12 needs, resolved above.
