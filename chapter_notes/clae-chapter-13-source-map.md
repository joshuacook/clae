(course lessons: none; case studies / data: sensor_readings; repos: none). This map

## Gaps and conflicts

- **The entire chapter is net-new.** Repo-wide and course-wide search for `kalman`,
  `state.space`, `recursi*`, `process noise`, `measurement noise`, and `innovation`
  returns **zero hits** in every course lesson `*_math.md` (001-014), every ILA chapter
  (`introduction_to_linear_algebra/ch/*`), and every clae-refs notebook (HMD, PCAt). The
  few `grep` hits on "filter"/"signal" in the refs are data-filtering / signal-vs-noise
  usage, not filtering *theory*. There is no scrap to adapt for 13.1-13.4 or 13.6. This
  is the cleanest scratch chapter in the book.

- **No course lesson reaches this material, and none was ever planned to.** Lessons
  001-014 terminate at PCA implementation. The course's planned-but-unwritten estimation
  lessons (17-20: CLT, LLN, linear estimators, MMSE) stop at *static* MMSE; even had they
  been produced, none covers recursion, state-space, or Kalman filtering. Ch 13 has no
  course ancestor at all.

- **sensor_readings does not carry temporal dynamics (the central data finding).** The
  dataset is generated by `generate_scale_varying_data` in
  `assessments/project-1/generate_datasets.py`: 1000 rows x 10 columns of **i.i.d.
  standard-Gaussian noise** (`np.random.randn`) multiplied by a fixed per-column scale
  ladder `np.logspace(-2, 2, 10)`. Confirmed empirically: column standard deviations run
  ~0.01, 0.028, 0.076, 0.21, 0.61, 1.73, 4.78, 13.3, 35.5, 94.5 (a clean decade ladder),
  and there is **no row-to-row (time) correlation, no underlying state, no trajectory** to
  track. The "1000 timestamps" label in the dataset README and the column-as-sensor framing
  are presentation only; statistically the rows are exchangeable white noise. The dataset
  was authored for the **SVD/PCA scale-normalization** story (project-1), not for filtering.
  A Kalman filter run on it would estimate a constant level corrupted by white measurement
  noise (the degenerate "tracking a constant" case) and would converge to the static LMMSE
  estimate, which is pedagogically fine as a *first* example but cannot demonstrate
  filtering, prediction through motion, or smoothing. **Resolution needed at outline time:**
  either (a) treat sensor_readings as the "constant-state" smoke test and **synthesize a
  small companion series with real dynamics** (a random-walk or constant-velocity state plus
  measurement noise) for the substantive 13.5 demo, matching the synthetic-data convention
  already used elsewhere in the book; or (b) re-derive sensor_readings with an AR/state model.
  Recommend (a): keep sensor_readings as the named through-line asset for continuity across
  Ch 12-14, but generate the trackable trajectory the filter actually needs.

- **In-book dependency Ch 12 -> Ch 13 is the real "source."** Ch 13 has no external source;
  its upstream is the book itself. The Ch 12 capstone builds the **static** LMMSE estimator
  on sensor_readings (outline 12.6, "the Estimator seed"); Ch 13 collects on it by making
  that estimator recursive. Sections 13.1 and 13.3 must be written assuming the Ch 12
  orthogonality principle and LMMSE derivation are in hand, and 13.4's Kalman gain should be
  presented as the recursive incarnation of the Ch 12 LMMSE gain. Coordinate notation with
  Ch 12 (estimate, error covariance, gain) so the recursion reads as a continuation, not a
  restart. Forward dependency: Ch 13's scalar filter is extended to the full multivariate
  filter in Ch 14.6 (the Estimator finale), so keep 13.4 scalar and flag the matrix
  generalization as Ch 14's job.

- **No implementation to port.** No Kalman/recursive code exists in any source, so 13.5 is
  written from scratch in numpy (consistent with the book's stack). HMD/PCAt notebooks offer
  only data-loading patterns, not filtering.

## Implication for the chapter outline

The section sequence holds and the scratch tag is accurate (and, unlike Ch 5, not
understated: there is genuinely no reusable prose scrap, only one dataset asset with a
caveat). Net read:

- **13.1-13.4** are fully net-new theory, written from the Ch 12 LMMSE result rather than
  from any course/repo source. 13.3 (recursive estimation) is the load-bearing pivot and
  should be derived explicitly as "Ch 12 LMMSE, done online"; 13.4 presents the scalar
  Kalman predict-update cycle as the recursion's closed form, with the Kalman gain shown to
  be the Ch 12 LMMSE gain in sequential dress. Keep the filter **scalar**; defer the
  multivariate recursions to Ch 14.1/14.6.

- **13.5** anchors on sensor_readings for continuity but should **not** rely on it for the
  dynamics demo: as established above the dataset is scale-varying white noise with no state
  to track. Plan to (a) use a single sensor_readings column as the "estimate a constant in
  noise" warm-up that shows the filter converging to the static LMMSE answer, and (b)
  synthesize a companion random-walk / constant-velocity trajectory with measurement noise to
  demonstrate true filtering, prediction, and the predict-update cycle. Flag this as a data
  task for the chapter, not an assumed asset.

- **13.6** is write-from-blank (no exercise seed anywhere).

Net assessment: Ch 13 is the book's purest scratch chapter. Zero external source for any
section; the only inputs are (1) the in-book Ch 12 static LMMSE estimator, which Ch 13
makes recursive, and (2) the sensor_readings dataset, which provides a streaming-log shape
but **not** the temporal dynamics a filter needs and must be supplemented with a synthesized
trajectory. The chapter is where the book's estimation arc goes dynamic, and it pays its
source debt entirely from prior chapters and from new writing, then hands the multivariate
generalization forward to Ch 14.
