# Further treks — end-of-book stock (ruled 2026-07-12)

Chapters carry no treks sections; stock accumulates here and consolidates into
one annotated back-matter reading trail.

## From Ch 1

- Strang, *Introduction to Linear Algebra* + the filmed 18.06 lectures (OCW):
  the constructive tradition, from the source.
- Axler, *Linear Algebra Done Right*: the axiomatic road not taken; a beautiful
  second course.
- Lockhart, *A Mathematician's Lament*: why the first pass went the way it did.
- Singer, *Linearity, Symmetry, and Prediction in the Hydrogen Atom*: where
  "orbitals are a basis" stops being a teaser (after Ch 3).
- Lawson, Hanson, Kincaid, Krogh, "Basic Linear Algebra Subprograms for Fortran
  Usage," ACM TOMS 5(3), 1979: the paper that named axpy.

## From Ch 2

- Strang, *Computational Science and Engineering*: the book built around K.
- Cook, *Computational Methods in Molecular Quantum Mechanics*, Leanpub 2016:
  the author's first walk through K's eigenproblem.
- Gelman, "Scaling regression inputs by dividing by two standard deviations,"
  Statistics in Medicine 27(15), 2008: the four-page argument behind X_g.
- Harris et al., "Array programming with NumPy," Nature 585, 2020: how the
  array actually lives in memory.
